Every week we select a new featured member from our community who has gone above and beyond to support the community and actively talks with everyone. As a special thanks for being purely amazing, we want to showcase them in our Discord server and Website!

This weeks featured member is

LukeDimond

A little about the Featured Member:

How long have you been streaming for?

It will be two years in December!

What got you into streaming?

Honestly, I had not a clue when I first got started so was basically just streaming so my friends could watch.

What do you normally play/do on your channel?

Mostly shooter and BR games, but I'm currently revisiting my variety roots and doing some IRL and music stuff as well!

What got you into gaming?

My father built me my first gaming PC when I was 7 years old. It had windows 98 and 256mb of RAM hha. After he passed I carried on the legacy. Runs in the family!

What was the first game you played?

It was a game called carnivores I believe. A dinosaur hunting game. Looking back, the graphics were so miserable haha.

What is your all time favorite game?

Runescape is a game that really helped me get more immersed into both gaming, and Twitch. So I think in a nostalgic way it takes the cake.

What do you do for fun off stream?

Off stream I'm a music producer and songwriter, I do that whenever I'm not off of the stream. As of recently I've been doing a lot of music for TV & Film.

How did you get your Twitch name?

I just underwent a rebrand from "dukelimond" to "lukedimond". I got the first name by switching my first and last name's first letters back in the AIM days. But just decided to switch to my full and legal name due to branding decisions and keeping it simple!

What brought you to the community?

Noodles came to me with a vision one day and brought me on in the beginning originally as an admin and behind the scenes business guy. So I got to see his vision blossom from the beginning, which was rad!

What's your favorite thing about Twitch Crew?

Unity and the fact that when the meetups happen, it doesn't feel like a networking event and more of a family gathering. Networking is cool and all, but TC is much much bigger than that.
